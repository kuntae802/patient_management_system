A genuinely strong spine pair: flow coverage, component pairing, state coverage, and inheritance discipline are near-airtight, and the spine/source/decision-log trace is exemplary. A consumer can source-extract the clinical surface cleanly — every {components.*} ref in EXPERIENCE.md resolves to a defined DESIGN.md token, every UJ has a named-protagonist flow, every IA surface has its state treatments. Two issues block a clean extract: (1) EXPERIENCE.md defers contrast numbers to DESIGN.md but DESIGN.md states no contrast/WCAG target anywhere — a dangling promise on load-bearing color combos; (2) mockup inline-linking is one aggregate nickname reference instead of per-section links to the six filenames, so visual-reference resolution is weak. Everything else, on the rubric axis, is polish.

The two additional adversarial reviewers shift the picture materially, and the rubric's "polish" framing does not survive contact with them. Accessibility is THIN, not done: the team solved colorblindness and cheap monitors thoroughly and then declared accessibility complete — but there is no screen-reader / ARIA / semantics layer anywhere in the spine or mockups (the safety-critical allergy alert, 409 toasts, and the audited RRN reveal are all unannounced), keyboard operability is asserted but unspecified and contradicted by the mockups, and multiple load-bearing text colors fail WCAG AA. That review carries 4 criticals. Clinical-safety is ADEQUATE but with 2 criticals that must close before build — most damningly, the flagship encounter-hub mock flags 김영희 as 아스피린-allergic while the patient app shows her a completed 아스피린 100mg prescription, and the spine has no allergy-vs-order cross-check (only same-ingredient duplicate warning, FR-052); the flagship artifact demonstrates the exact accident the system is meant to prevent. The second clinical critical is printed-RRN on statutory forms bypassing the decrypt/audit gate with no print-PII policy at all. Net: the contract is extractable, but it is not buildable as a safe, accessible clinical product without closing the six criticals and nineteen highs below.

Accessibility review (adversarial)

Thin review-accessibility.md

The team did real, above-average accessibility thinking on exactly one axis — the "no-meaning-by-color/shadow-alone" redundant encoding (color + glyph + weight + pattern), and it largely holds. But that is roughly one of WCAG's ~50 success criteria, and the spine treats it as if it were the whole job. The moment you look past it, the floor drops out: no screen-reader/semantics layer exists at all (zero ARIA roles, labels, live regions, or <label> associations anywhere); the mockups are accessibility-hostile as built (icon-only buttons, controls built from <span>/<div>); concrete contrast failures in load-bearing text; "keyboard-first" is asserted but never specified; reduced-motion, text-scaling floor, and form-error/AT association are entirely unaddressed. The Accessibility Floor section is five bullets, four of which are the color/shadow point restated — accessibility here is a slogan ("음영 비의존"), not a coverage plan. Severity: 4 critical · 9 high · 13 medium · 11 low (37 total).

critical

No ARIA / semantics / AT story anywhere

§5; all 6 mockups; EXPERIENCE.md Accessibility Floor
Search of all six mockups: the only aria-* is aria-hidden="true" on decorative sparklines. No role, no aria-label, no aria-live, no aria-checked, no <label for>. EXPERIENCE.md's Accessibility Floor does not mention screen readers, AT, ARIA, or semantics at all. For a legally and clinically sensitive system (RRN, allergies, prescriptions, audit), shipping with no AT story is the highest-impact failure here.
Fix: add a screen-reader/semantics layer to the spine; require real roles, labels, and live regions across components before any accessibility claim.

The allergy/safety alert (alert-row) has no role="alert"/aria-live — the single most safety-critical element on the screen is not announced to AT. A3 badges render as a sighted-only <span><dot/><lbl>진행중</lbl></span> construct.
Fix: allergy/safety alert must be role="alert" or a live region; status changes that matter (realtime row updates) need polite live regions.
critical

Masked RRN reveal: control unlabeled + revealed value + audit event not announced

high

Placeholder & disabled text fails contrast everywhere

§2; --text-disabled #97A9AA
#97A9AA on white = 2.45:1 (on surface-muted 2.22:1) — below even the 3:1 large-text floor. high

Clinical-safety + PII/security review (adversarial)

Adequate review-clinical-safety.md

The spine has genuinely strong instincts: allergy alert is a defined component with monitor-robust styling, RRN masks by default, reveal is audit-labeled, RBAC is 3-layered, audit is append-only, the state-machine "only-allowed-transitions" rule is stated, and 409/403/422 user-facing behavior is fully specified (a real strength). But almost every patient-safety guarantee is asserted as a principle and demonstrated once in a mockup, with no spec for the failure modes that actually cause harm: allergy overflow, prescribing against a known allergen, order-loss visibility, identity confirmation, and several PII leak surfaces (printed RRN, error/realtime payloads, deep links). The mockups even contain a live contradiction that demonstrates the exact harm the system is supposed to prevent. "Decoration in one mock" vs "systematic pattern" is the recurring weakness. Severity: 2 critical · 8 high · 8 medium · 3 low (~20 distinct).

critical

Mockups prescribe a drug the same patient is flagged allergic to — no allergy↔order cross-check

§1; key-encounter-hub.html L631,746 + key-patient-app.html L540–546
김영희 carries 아스피린 알레르기 in the encounter hub, yet the patient app shows her a completed prescription for 아스피린 100mg with cheerful dosing copy. The only drug-safety check the spine names is 동일성분 중복 처방 경고 (FR-052). There is no allergy-vs-order check anywhere in DESIGN.md or EXPERIENCE.md — the "와파린 병용 경고" is hand-placed text in one alert chip, decoration in one mock, not a systematic pattern. The flagship artifact demonstrates the accident.
Fix: add an explicit clinical-safety component to order-panel + 임상 안전 패턴: on every order add, evaluate against (a) recorded allergies, (b) current meds for interactions, and surface a hard-stop / override-with-reason dialog. Define it as a spine pattern. Fix the demo data so the golden-path patient isn't prescribed her own allergen.
critical

Printed 영수증 shows RRN — statutory print path bypasses the reveal/audit gate; no print-PII policy

§4; key-billing.html L704, L667
The legal preview prints 주민등록번호 710314-2******. Two problems: (a) if the statutory form requires the full RRN, printing it is an un-audited reveal outside the "표시 + 감사기록" gate — the entire decrypt/audit discipline is bypassed by the print path; (b) if it stays masked, the document may be non-conformant. Either way the spine never addresses PII on printed output (paper on a desk, "PDF 저장" to a shared drive) — a classic leak vector.
